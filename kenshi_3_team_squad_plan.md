# Kenshi Squad Plan — 3 Teams of 12

Version: 2026-05-24  
Scope: vanilla Kenshi, unique recruits only, plus your custom main character.  
Assumption: Team 1 is the elite roaming/adventure squad; Team 2 is the self-sustaining production/crafting city team; Team 3 is the static defence and turret team.

---

## Key assumptions

- **Team 1** includes **you** and **Beep**.
- **Burn stays in Team 1**, as requested.
- This is based on **starting usefulness and intended role**, not only long-term maximum potential.
- In vanilla Kenshi, **Meitou** is the highest weapon grade, but it is not craftable without mods.
- In vanilla Kenshi, player weapon crafting can normally reach high grades, but **Edge Type 3 and Meitou weapons are not craftable without modding**.
- The best general-purpose endgame weapon target for the elite squad is usually the **Meitou Falling Sun**, because it has exceptional damage potential and is lighter than the other heavy weapons.
- Strength requirements matter. A character should not be given heavy weapons too early unless their Strength is high enough, otherwise they will fight slowly.

## Team 1 weapon rationale

### You
Recommended path: **Falling Sun path**, unless you prefer a safer defensive captain build.

- Early: Sabre or Polearm, because they are safer and easier to use.
- Mid-game: Plank or Falling Sun once Strength and Toughness are acceptable.
- Endgame: **Meitou Falling Sun**.

Why: the Falling Sun is one of the strongest all-round heavy weapons in the game. It has exceptional damage potential and is lighter than other heavy weapons, making it more practical than the Fragment Axe for most elite fighters.

### Beep
Recommended path: **Katana/Nodachi first, Falling Sun later only if heavily trained**.

- Early: Katana, Ninja Blade, or Nodachi to train Dexterity quickly.
- Mid-game: Sabre if you want him to survive longer.
- Endgame: Beep can eventually carry a Falling Sun, but only after serious Strength and Toughness training.

Why: Beep starts weak. Give him a weapon that trains Dexterity and lets him participate without being completely crippled by weight. He can become anything, but he should not start as a heavy weapons user.

### Bo
Recommended path: **light blade / stealth weapon**.

- Early: Ninja Blade or Wakizashi.
- Mid-game: Katana or Ninja Blade.
- Endgame: **Meitou Ninja Blade** or **Meitou Katana**.

Why: Bo is better used as an elite scout, assassin, lockpicker, field medic and robotics support. A light weapon keeps her mobile and aligned with stealth/assassination work.

### Agnu
Recommended path: **Heavy Weapon**.

- Early: Plank or Falling Sun if Strength supports it.
- Mid-game: Falling Sun.
- Endgame: **Meitou Fragment Axe** only if Strength is extremely high; otherwise **Meitou Falling Sun**.

Why: Agnu’s starting Strength makes heavy weapons tempting. The Fragment Axe can be brutal but requires very high Strength to use efficiently. Falling Sun is the more practical elite option for most of the game.

### Kang
Recommended path: **Heavy Weapon frontline**.

- Early: Plank or Falling Sun.
- Mid-game: Falling Sun.
- Endgame: **Meitou Falling Sun**.

Why: Kang is one of the stronger Shek recruits and fits perfectly as a heavy frontline damage dealer.

### Rane the Giant
Recommended path: **Heavy Weapon frontline**.

- Early: Plank.
- Mid-game: Falling Sun.
- Endgame: **Meitou Falling Sun**.

Why: same logic as Kang. Rane should become a high-impact frontline fighter.

### Seto
Recommended path: **Heavy Weapon or Hacker**.

- Early: Heavy Polearm, Paladin’s Cross or Plank.
- Mid-game: Paladin’s Cross or Falling Sun.
- Endgame: **Meitou Falling Sun** for general combat, or **Meitou Paladin’s Cross** for anti-robot/skeleton duty.

Why: Seto is a strong unique recruit but comes later. She can be shaped into either another heavy frontliner or a specialist against mechanical enemies.

### Griffin
Recommended path: **Hacker / Paladin’s Cross**.

- Early: Combat Cleaver or Paladin’s Cross.
- Mid-game: Paladin’s Cross.
- Endgame: **Meitou Paladin’s Cross**.

Why: Griffin is a good all-rounder. Hackers are excellent utility weapons, especially against armour, robots and skeletons. Paladin’s Cross gives him a clear specialist role instead of just making him another Falling Sun user.

### Chad
Recommended path: **Martial Arts main, small sidearm**.

- Early: Martial Arts training, plus Wakizashi as backup.
- Mid-game: Martial Arts and Dodge focus.
- Endgame: **Martial Arts** as primary. Sidearm: **Meitou Wakizashi**.

Why: Chad has good Unarmed/Dodge potential. In Kenshi, Martial Arts can become extremely powerful, but it requires training and toughness.

### Infinite Wingwang
Recommended path: **Sabre specialist**.

- Early: Foreign Sabre or Desert Sabre.
- Mid-game: higher-grade Sabre.
- Endgame: **Meitou Foreign Sabre** or **Meitou Desert Sabre**.

Why: he already has Sabre skill. Sabres give him a defensive and reliable frontline/skirmisher role without competing with every Shek for the same heavy weapon.

### Luquin
Recommended path: **Ninja Blade / Katana / Topper**.

- Early: Ninja Blade or Katana.
- Mid-game: Topper or Katana.
- Endgame: **Meitou Ninja Blade** for stealth/assassin role or **Meitou Topper** for a stronger mixed combat role.

Why: Luquin’s stealth and assassination profile makes him ideal for infiltration, knockouts, scouting and surgical strikes.

### Burn
Recommended path: **Polearm first, Falling Sun later if trained**.

- Early: Polearm or Heavy Polearm.
- Mid-game: Heavy Polearm or Plank.
- Endgame: **Meitou Falling Sun** if trained; otherwise high-grade Polearm/Heavy Polearm.

Why: Burn is a skeleton and therefore excellent for dangerous expeditions. Polearms are practical and safer while he develops Strength and combat skills. The Falling Sun is the eventual elite target if you want him as a true frontline monster.

---

## Team 1 acquisition priorities

1. **Practical first target:** buy or craft high-grade weapons that match each training path.
2. **Best general endgame target:** collect **Meitou** weapons.
3. **Top priority Meitou for the squad:** **Falling Sun**.
4. **Do not over-equip too early:** a Meitou heavy weapon on an undertrained fighter can perform worse than a lighter, lower-grade weapon because of attack-speed penalties.

| 11 | **Bard** | Leather/textile worker | Clothing bench / armour support | Armour Smithing via clothing, labouring |
| 12 | **Reva** | Robotics crafter | Repair kits / skeleton limb production | Robotics, Science, Labouring |

## Team 2 production model

### Food and survival chain
- **Miu**: farms, crop management and food supply.
- **Green Finger**: secondary farming and field labour.
- **Jewel**: cooking, ration production and food storage.
- **Yamdu**: overall production lead and emergency support anywhere in the chain.

Recommended base jobs:
- Farming
- Wells / water hauling
- Cooking stove
- Food storage
- Animal feeder if you use pack animals

### Construction and infrastructure chain
- **Soman**: chief engineer.
- **Yamdu**: senior engineer and build-speed accelerator.
- **Pia**: hauls building materials and supplies.
- **Hobbs**: stone, iron and general labour.

Recommended base jobs:
- Stone mine
- Stone processor
- Iron resource
- Iron refinery
- Steel refinery
- Building material production
- Electrical components as needed

### Weapon crafting chain
- **Cat**: primary Weapon Smith.

Why Cat: Cat has useful Medic/Science foundations but does not have a stronger natural production niche than the others. Weapon Smithing has to be trained by active weapon crafting, so Cat can become the dedicated long-term weapon specialist.

Recommended setup:
- Weapon Smithy
- Weapon storage
- Iron/steel supply
- Good lighting around crafting stations
- Keep Cat fully healed before long crafting sessions

Important vanilla note:
- You can craft excellent weapons, but **Edge Type 3 and Meitou are not craftable in vanilla without mods**.
- Endgame crafted weapons are still very useful for equipping the settlement and secondary squads.

### Armour crafting chain
- **Doctor Chung**: primary Armour Smith.
- **Bard**: clothing, leather and textile support.

Why Doctor Chung: he has strong Medic value and can remain in the base. Armour Smithing is an excellent long-term specialisation because high-quality armour is essential for both Team 1 and Team 3.

Recommended setup:
- Clothing Bench for early Armour Smithing training
- Leather Armour Crafting Bench
- Heavy Armour Smithy
- Fabric Loom
- Leather Tanning Bench
- Armour storage
- Good lighting

Training approach:
- Start with clothing/light armour for skill gain.
- Move into leather and heavier armour as materials and research improve.
- Aim for masterwork armour production for the whole faction.

### Crossbow and bolt crafting chain
- **Izumi**: Crossbow Smith and research/science lead.

Why Izumi: she already has Science/Medic usefulness and can naturally sit in the technical production chain. Crossbow Smithing is trained by crafting crossbows and bolts.

Recommended setup:
- Crossbow Crafting Bench
- Arrow Making Bench
- Bolt storage near turret walls
- Crossbow Parts / Hinges / Spring Steel supply

Production priority:
1. Bolts for Team 3.
2. Crossbows for Green and Headshot.
3. Spare crossbows for future recruits.

### Robotics and skeleton support chain
- **Reva**: Robotics crafter and repair-kit producer.

Recommended setup:
- Robotics Bench
- Skeleton Repair Kit production
- Robotics components
- Limb crafting once researched
- Repair bed access if available

Why this matters:
- Team 1 has **Agnu** and **Burn**, so reliable skeleton repair support is important.
- If you use limb-loss gameplay heavily, Robotics becomes strategically important for the whole faction.

### Logistics and hauling
- **Pia**: primary runner and logistics coordinator.
- **Hobbs**: heavy labour and mining.
- **Bard**: textile/leather hauling.
- **Green Finger**: farm/resource bridge.

Recommended jobs:
- Haul to storage
- Haul water
- Haul wheatstraw/greenfruit/cactus/riceweed depending on biome
- Haul iron plates and steel bars
- Haul fabric/leather
- Maintain bolt storage near defences

| 3 | **Cornelius** | Animal defender / chaos response | Animal combat | Keep near inner gate or food stores |

| 5 | **Hamut** | Gate guard | Hacker / Sabre | Strong anti-slaver flavour |
| 6 | **Crumblejon** | Heavy gate guard | Plank / Sabre / Polearm | Good Strength base |
| 7 | **Ruka** | Frontline defender | Heavy Polearm / Sabre | Reliable free Shek guard |
| 8 | **Ray** | Gate bruiser | Heavy Polearm / Hacker | Soldier Drone guard |
| 9 | **Silvershade** | Utility defender | Polearm / crossbow backup | Can support turrets/logistics |
| 10 | **Sinklyde** | Wall patrol | Polearm / Sabre | General defender |
| 11 | **Knife** | Wall patrol | Katana / Sabre | General defender |
| 12 | **Oron** | Wall patrol | Polearm / Sabre | General defender |

## Team 3 defensive doctrine

### Outer wall
- **Green** and **Headshot** stay on the best turrets.
- Keep bolt storage close to the wall.
- Give both good crossbows so they remain useful when moving off-turret.

### Gate line
- **Stubs Momuso**, **Hamut**, **Crumblejon**, **Ruka** and **Ray** should hold the gate.
- Prefer armour and toughness over speed.
- Sabres and polearms are practical for defence; hackers are useful against armoured enemies and skeletons.

### Inner response
- **Cornelius** stays inside or near the inner gate.
- **Silvershade**, **Sinklyde**, **Knife** and **Oron** act as mobile responders, medics-in-training, haulers during peace and wall patrol during raids.

### Equipment priorities for Team 3
1. Good armour before fancy weapons.
2. Turrets and crossbow bolts before melee upgrades.
3. Gate guards need helmets and stomach/chest protection.
4. Give everyone basic first aid kits.
5. Keep spare food, bolts and repair kits close to defence positions.

# Strategic progression plan

## Early game

Team 1:
- Recruit Beep, Burn and early strong fighters.
- Train Strength, Toughness, Athletics and core weapon skills.
- Do not rush heavy weapons too early.

Team 2:
- Establish food, water, storage and basic materials.
- Assign permanent jobs early so skills grow passively.
- Start Cat on low-grade weapon crafting and Doctor Chung/Bard on clothing/armour training.

Team 3:
- Build walls, gate and first turret line.
- Place Green and Headshot on turrets as soon as recruited.
- Use weaker unique recruits as guards while they train.

## Mid game

Team 1:
- Move stronger fighters to Falling Sun, Paladin’s Cross and specialised Meitou targets where possible.
- Use Bo and Luquin for stealth operations.
- Use Burn and Agnu for dangerous environmental zones.

Team 2:
- Expand into:
  - Weapon Smithing
  - Armour Smithing
  - Crossbow Smithing
  - Robotics
  - Research
  - Steel production
  - Fabric/leather chains

Team 3:
- Upgrade turrets.
- Upgrade armour.
- Create a gate kill-zone.
- Maintain a reserve of bolts and medical supplies.

## Late game

Team 1:
- Hunt Meitou weapons.
- Standardise elite armour.
- Push high-risk ruins, faction leaders and world-state targets.

Team 2:
- Produce masterwork armour where possible.
- Produce high-grade weapons for non-Team-1 members.
- Produce robotics and repair kits for skeletons and prosthetic users.

Team 3:
- Upgrade to a layered defence model:
  - Outer turret wall
  - Gate guard squad
  - Inner fallback point
  - Emergency medical station
  - Bolt and food storage close to combat points
