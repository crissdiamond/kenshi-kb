# Starting from The Hub — Practical Route to the Squin-Side HQ

## Objective

You spawned in **The Hub** and want to establish the planned HQ near **Squin**, ideally south / south-east of Squin towards the Border Zone–Swamp edge, where the long-term base can support farming, crafting, mining, and defence.

The priority is **survival and controlled progression**, not rushing into base building. In *Kenshi*, an early outpost attracts raids and can become harder to defend than simply operating from a town. The plan below uses The Hub and Squin as staging areas before committing to the HQ.

Sources checked: Kenshi Wiki pages for The Hub, Border Zone and outpost building; Kenshi Wiki money-making guidance; community guidance on early copper mining and Squin/The Hub starts. Links are listed at the end.

## High-level strategy

| Phase | Main goal | Base status | Why |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Survive The Hub start | No base | The Hub is central but weak; use it only as a temporary staging point. |
| 2 | Move to Squin | No base | Squin is safer, has better shops, Shek recruits and nearby copper/iron. |
| 3 | Recruit early Team 1 / Team 2 / Team 3 members | No base or town house only | Build squad strength before exposing yourself to raids. |
| 4 | Prospect south / south-east of Squin | No permanent build yet | Confirm iron, copper, stone, water and fertility before committing. |
| 5 | Buy/build minimal staging infrastructure | Prefer Squin house first | Store research, materials and recruits safely. |
| 6 | Found HQ only when ready | Small defended outpost | Build once you have workers, guards, food, building materials and basic research. |

## Phase 1 — First days in The Hub

| Step | Action | Details | Risk | Exit condition |
|---:|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Pause and check inventory | Confirm food, medkit, money and starting stats. Do not fight yet unless unavoidable. | Low | You know what you can sell or keep. |
| 2 | Find the bar/trader | Buy basic food if needed. Avoid spending everything unless you find a key recruit. | Low | You have enough food for the short walk to Squin. |
| 3 | Mine copper near The Hub if safe | Copper mining is a standard early money method. Mine close enough to retreat if bandits appear. | Medium | You have enough money for food, first aid and at least one recruit or backpack. |
| 4 | Use guards and town safety | If attacked, run to town guards or gate areas rather than trying to win. | Medium | Main character survives and gains some athletics/toughness from controlled danger. |
| 5 | Do not build an outpost yet | A player outpost can trigger raids and hostile attention. Early on, that is a liability. | High if ignored | You leave The Hub without founding a base. |

### Minimum recommended before leaving The Hub

| Item / condition | Minimum | Comfortable |
|---|---:|---:|
| Food | 2–3 days | 4–5 days |
| Medkits | 1 basic kit | 2 basic kits |
| Cats | Enough for supplies | Enough for 1 recruit if found |
| Athletics | Any improvement helps | Character can outrun some starving bandits |
| Encumbrance | Light | Light or moderate only |

## Phase 2 — Move from The Hub to Squin

| Step | Action | Details | Risk | Notes |
|---:|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Leave The Hub for Squin | Squin is also in the Border Zone and is one of the key nearby settlements. | Medium | Travel during daylight if visibility helps you. |
| 2 | Avoid fights on the road | Starving Bandits and Dust Bandits can overwhelm a solo starter. | Medium | Run, do not duel. |
| 3 | Use stealth while travelling if needed | Stealth training while moving can help long term, but do not crawl slowly into danger. | Low/Medium | Toggle based on threat. |
| 4 | Enter Squin and stabilise | Sell copper/loot, buy food, buy medkits, check recruit options. | Low | Squin becomes your first real staging town. |

### Why Squin first?

Squin is a stronger support town than The Hub for this plan. It gives you access to Shek-area recruits, shops, safer recovery, and nearby mining opportunities. The Border Zone is also useful because The Hub, Squin and a Tech Hunter Waystation are all in relatively close reach.

## Phase 4 — Exact HQ scouting route from Squin

The intended HQ is **not inside Squin** and not immediately beside the town. You are looking for a practical outpost site **south or south-east of Squin**, ideally towards the Border Zone–Swamp edge.

| Scouting step | What to do | What to look for | Accept / reject logic |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Leave Squin heading south / south-east | Stay on the Border Zone side first. | Avoid going too deep into the Swamp early. |
| 2 | Use Prospecting repeatedly | Check iron, copper, stone, water, fertility and wind. | Do not rely on visual judgement only. |
| 3 | Find iron and copper within workable range | Ideally one or both inside future walls, or just outside guarded gates. | Reject if mining is too far from the settlement. |
| 4 | Check stone quality | Stone is essential for building material production. | Prefer high stone; do not settle where stone is poor. |
| 5 | Check water and fertility | The Swamp edge improves farming potential. | Prefer enough water/fertility for farms without moving too deep into danger. |
| 6 | Check defensibility | Look for ridges, chokepoints or a compact build area. | Reject wide-open layouts that need huge walls early. |
| 7 | Mark mentally, but do not build yet | Return to Squin and prepare. | Build only when the squad and supplies are ready. |

### Resource target table

| Resource | Minimum acceptable | Ideal target | Why |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Iron | 80%+ nearby | 100% node nearby | Weapons, plates, building chain and training economy. |
| Copper | 60%+ nearby | 80–100% nearby | Early money and electrical components later. |
| Stone | 70%+ | 90–100% | Building materials for walls, houses and production. |
| Water | 50%+ | 80%+ | Farming and self-sufficiency. |
| Fertility | 50%+ | Mixed arid/swamp coverage | Supports food chain and later hemp/riceweed/cactus/wheat choices. |
| Wind | Useful | Strong enough for early wind power | Power is helpful, but batteries can smooth inconsistency. |

## Phase 5 — When to found the HQ

Do **not** found the HQ just because you have found the location. Found it when you can survive the consequences.

| Readiness area | Minimum before founding | Better before founding |
|---|---|---|
| Squad size | 5–6 people | 8–12 people |
| Combat | At least a few can survive bandits | Team 1 has several competent fighters |
| Workers | 2–3 labour/build workers | Dedicated Team 2 early workers |
| Medical | Several medkits | Dedicated medic/recovery stock |
| Food | Several days of food | Food production plan ready |
| Materials | Building materials and iron plates | Enough to build walls/gate/storage quickly |
| Research | Basic storage and production | Walls, crossbows/turrets, batteries, farming basics |
| Defence | Can retreat to Squin if needed | Gate, walls and defenders ready |

| Order | Build / setup | Purpose | Notes |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Small shack or storm house | Initial indoor work/storage | Keep the footprint small. |
| 2 | Storage: copper, iron, building materials | Prevent job chaos | Storage enables automated hauling. |
| 3 | Stone processor | Building material production | Essential for expansion. |
| 4 | Iron refinery / plate chain | Industry foundation | Needed for weapons, armour and construction. |
| 5 | Small farm trial | Test fertility and water assumptions | Do not overbuild farms before confirming yields. |
| 6 | Walls and one main gate | Defensive shape | Keep it compact. |
| 7 | Crossbow/turret positions | Defence multiplier | Team 3 becomes useful here. |
| 8 | Beds / recovery | Injury management | Critical for raids. |
| 9 | Dedicated crafting stations | Start weapon/armour/crossbow specialisation | Only once food and defence are stable. |

## Practical early-game rhythm

| Day range | Focus | What success looks like |
|---|---|---|
| Days 1–3 | Survive The Hub, mine cautiously, move to Squin | You reach Squin alive with food and some cats. |
| Days 3–8 | Mine near Squin, recruit first bodies, buy supplies | You have 3–5 people and can handle small threats or retreat safely. |
| Days 8–15 | Buy house/research/storage if possible; scout HQ site | You know the exact HQ location but have not overcommitted. |
| Days 15–25 | Gather materials, assign early Team 2/3 roles, build small outpost | The base has storage, walls/gate plan and a retreat strategy. |
| After Day 25 | Expand industry, farming, defence and longer recruitment routes | The HQ becomes sustainable rather than a liability. |

These day ranges are indicative only. If the run is harsh, delay the HQ. Kenshi punishes premature settlement more than slow preparation.

## Key risks and mitigations

| Risk | Why it matters | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Building too early | Outposts can attract raids and hostile attention. | Use Squin as staging first. |
| Going too deep into the Swamp | Blood Spiders and swamp threats can punish weak squads. | Build on the Border Zone side of the edge, not deep Swamp. |
| Overloading the main character | Encumbrance can stop you escaping. | Train strength carefully but keep travel speed viable. |
| Recruiting too many non-fighters early | Food and movement become harder. | Park workers in Squin or delay recruitment. |
| Triggering Anti-Slaver alignment too early | Makes UC/Trader routes harder. | Do UC-area recruitment and shopping before Bo route. |
| Wide base footprint | More wall/gate exposure and slower defence. | Start compact and expand later. |
