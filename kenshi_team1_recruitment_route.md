# Kenshi — Team 1 Recruitment Route

Purpose: build the **Team 1 adventuring squad** in a realistic order, using recruitment as the first strategic function of the run.  
Assumption: this is for a **vanilla Kenshi** playthrough starting with one custom main character, and Team 1 remains:

1. You / main character  
2. Beep  
3. Bo  
4. Agnu  
5. Kang  
6. Rane the Giant  
7. Seto  
8. Griffin  
9. Chad  
10. Infinite Wingwang  
11. Luquin  
12. Burn  

The order below is not a pure “strongest first” ranking. It balances:

- early-game survival;
- travel distance and danger;
- recruitment cost;
- whether a fight, prison break, faction condition, or world-state change is required;
- the need to let the main character and early recruits gain Athletics, Toughness, combat experience and money before attempting harder recruits.

## Suggested route logic

### Phase 1 — Build a safe early core

Target: **Griffin → Kang → Rane the Giant**.

This gives the main character three competent early fighters without requiring a boss fight, prison break or faction war. The main gating factor is money. During this phase, focus on:

- Athletics for everyone;
- Toughness through controlled fights and recovery;
- basic money-making;
- first aid and carrying capacity;
- avoiding permanent faction consequences.

### Phase 2 — The Mongrel expedition

Target: **Beep → Infinite Wingwang**.

Do this once your squad can travel fast and survive mistakes. You are not going to Mongrel because it is easy; you are going because it is strategically efficient: Beep and Infinite Wingwang are both there, and Beep is a core Team 1 identity recruit.

### Phase 3 — Strengthen the expeditionary squad

Target: **Burn → Chad**.

Burn adds Skeleton resilience and long-range expedition value. Chad adds another strong combatant, but you need to travel to Flats Lagoon and then equip him properly.

### Phase 4 — Hard rescue operations

Target: **Agnu → Luquin**.

These are not normal bar recruitments. They are operations. Bring:

- lockpicking capability;
- stealth/assassination if available;
- food;
- first aid;
- Skeleton repair kits;
- sleeping bags;
- backup mercenaries or Tech Hunters if your squad is still underpowered.

### Phase 5 — Political / faction recruits

Target: **Bo → Seto**.

Bo and Seto should be treated as strategic campaign milestones. Bo pulls you towards anti-slaver conflict. Seto requires major Shek Kingdom relationship progress, defeating or imprisoning major figures, or very high Shek relations.
